Supreme

Founded: 1994  •  Origin: Manhattan, NYC

Supreme is an American clothing brand established in Manhattan, New York in April 1994. The company focuses on streetwear, skateboarding, and hip hop fashion trends. In December 2020, VF Corporation bought Supreme for $2.1 billion. In July 2024, Supreme was sold to EssilorLuxottica for $1.5 billion.

History (condensed)

Founded by James Jebbia in 1994, Supreme’s red box logo draws inspiration from Barbara Kruger’s artwork. The Lafayette Street store in Manhattan was designed to accommodate skate culture and employ skaters — helping the brand earn credibility early on. Over the next two decades Supreme added locations (Los Angeles, London, Paris, Tokyo, etc.), high-visibility collaborations and legal battles over its box-logo.

Timeline / Milestones

  • 1994: Lafayette St store opens (NYC).
  • 2004: LA store on North Fairfax opens.
  • 2011–2016: London (2011), Paris (2016), several Tokyo locations.
  • 2017: ~50% stake sold to The Carlyle Group.
  • 2020: VF Corporation acquisition.
  • 2024: Sale to EssilorLuxottica.

Brand lore & practice

Limited drops, artist remixes (both licensed and unlicensed), celebrity visibility, and the “scarcity + hype” model are central to the Supreme story. The red box logo became a cultural symbol beyond clothing — one of streetwear’s most recognized marks.
