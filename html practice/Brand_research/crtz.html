Corteiz CRTZ — Rules The World

Founded: 2017  •  Origin: London, UK

Corteiz (commonly abbreviated CRTZ) is a London-based streetwear brand founded in 2017 by British-Nigerian entrepreneur and creative director Clint Ogbenna (also known as “Clint 419”). The label is known for its Alcatraz-inspired logo and strong, community-driven, guerrilla-style marketing that has built a cult following in the U.K. and beyond.

Origin & ethos

Starting with limited screen-printed tees and crewnecks, Corteiz cultivated an anti-establishment identity: the Alcatraz motif (a reference to the famous prison island) is used as a symbol of rebellion and outsider culture. The brand operates with scarcity-first drops, selective distribution, and a password-protected website approach that amplifies hype and community participation.

Milestones & notable actions

  • 2017: Corteiz founded in London; initial releases were limited t-shirts and hoodies.
  • 2021: The brand executed guerrilla drops in Soho and other locations, using off-beat activations to amplify scarcity and buzz.
  • 2022: "Da Great Bolo Exchange" and the "99p Market Stall" — two headline stunts where Corteiz exchanged high-end outerwear or sold cargos for 99 pence, driving viral attention and large turnouts. (Collected items were donated in some cases.)
  • 2023: Corteiz launched collaborations including a high-profile Nike Air Max 95 drop and other coop work that brought the brand into international sneaker/streetwear conversations.
  • 2023–2024: The brand expanded its presence with pop-ups and exchange events beyond London — including a notable denim exchange in New York — furthering its reputation for experiential marketing.

Practice & community

Rather than traditional PR and broad wholesale distribution, Corteiz relies on tight community curation — limited runs, surprise activations, and social media engagements orchestrated by the founder. This strategy turned the brand into a cultural touchstone for UK youth, endorsed organically by musicians and influencers.
