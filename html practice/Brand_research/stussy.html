Stüssy

Founded: Early 1980s  •  Origin: Laguna Beach, California

Stüssy is an American streetwear and lifestyle brand founded by Shawn Stüssy in the early 1980s. What began as hand-signed surfboards evolved into one of the foundational labels in streetwear — bridging surf, skate, hip-hop and high-fashion circles.

History

Shawn Stüssy shaped surfboards in Laguna Beach and wrote his surname on boards with a broad-tipped marker; that signature became the brand's logo when he began screen-printing T-shirts and apparel. The handwritten mark — inspired by family handwriting and graffiti vernacular — is instantly recognisable and helped the brand move from local surf shops to global retail.

Timeline / Milestones

  • Early 1980s: Shawn Stüssy begins marking shaped surfboards with his signature and creates early apparel runs.
  • 1984–1988: Distribution expands to international boutiques and specialty retailers.
  • 1990s–2000s: Stüssy embeds in skate and hip-hop culture and grows via collaborations.
  • 2010s–present: Ongoing collaborations and continued cultural relevance in both street and luxury contexts.

Identity

By the mid-to-late 1980s Stüssy had expanded beyond California into international specialty shops and boutiques across the U.S., Europe and Japan. Its cross-cultural appeal allowed it to grow organically through subcultures rather than traditional fashion cycles. Across decades Stüssy has collaborated with major players (Nike, Converse, G-Shock and more) and with designers and labels that sit between streetwear and luxury.
